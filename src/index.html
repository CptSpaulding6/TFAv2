Journey Through the World of a Web Design Student

Welcome to Guewen's portfolio, a young student studying Art at the Haute École Albert Jacquard, specializing in Web Design Transmedia. As you navigate through this website, you will discover his creations that reflect his journey.

avatar

Valet Guewen

After obtaining a degree in visual communication in France, this 19-year-old student decided to go to Belgium to continue his art studies at HEAJ.

After his first year, he chose to join the "Illustration and 2D Animation" section for 2 years before switching to the "Design Web Transmedia " section. Now 24 years old, he has acquired skills in numerous software programs, from layout and video editing to animation, photography, and web design.

Guewen's courses are varied and he is learning essential disciplines such as UX and UI design, as well as in-depth courses in HTML, CSS and JavaScript, which will enable him to understand and create interfaces with a focus on an optimal and enjoyable user experience.

Through numerous individual and group projects, he has had the opportunity to push back the boundaries of his creativity while gradually learning about the demands of the professional world.

First Step

in graphic design

After 2018, several internships in printing and communication agencies. Guewen was able to work on a number of occasions for professionals and produce various works.

These included the menu for a restaurant, a flyer and diploma for a giant cluedo, posters and t-shirts for a tattoo convention, as well as tarpaulins, flyers and a promotional video for a techno festival.

2020 2024

Catch the Light

From Passion to

Real-Life Experience

During an internship at a photo agency in 2018, Guewen discovered a new interest. As well as studio sessions, he accompanied his course master to several events, including a wedding, a christening, an equestrian competition, a sports competition, a fast-food restaurant and a restaurant.

It was a very interesting experience, because during the sports and equestrian competitions, Guewen was at the stand to print the photos live, on paper, on mugs, on t-shirts, caps and many other goodies. For events such as weddings, he also produced photo albums and video montages of the wedding. Or restaurant cards and posters with the actual visuals of the dishes.

"It was at this point that I bought my first camera, and at first I practised using it in nature during walks, then I continued to work with this photographer for events. Finally I was able to do my first professional session with the staff of a restaurant."
